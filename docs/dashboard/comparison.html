Level 1 — Executive summary across 9 tools

Eight high-level dimensions across nine tools. Honest framing: every claim cites a source; vendor claims are marked; unverified items are explicitly "unknown" rather than padded to "no".

Honesty rules applied to this page:
  • Every detection-rate number cites a public source (MITRE ATT&CK Evaluations, vendor whitepaper, or our own measurement).
  • Vendor-claimed numbers are marked “vendor claim” without endorsement.
  • xhelix numbers come from the May 2026 internal 482-test corpus (v1 measured) or stated as projections (v2 target).
  • Where a feature is “unknown” rather than “no,” that’s honest — we couldn’t verify from public docs.
  • Marketing language (“next-generation,” “AI-powered”) is excluded; only specific technical capabilities are compared.

What the Level-1 table is saying: Every commercial tool ships kernel observation and partial source attribution. None ship a tamper-evident forensic chain. None mediate credentials at the syscall (they detect theft after; xhelix denies the read). Aikido is in a different lane — it’s RASP, not host EDR — included to show xhelix is NOT a substitute for RASP, they’re complementary.
